What are the requirements for access to scaffolds?

Employers must provide access when the scaffold platforms are more than 2 feet (0.6 m) above or below a point of access.

Direct access is acceptable when the scaffold is not more than 14 inches (36 cm) horizontally and not more than 24 inches (61 cm) vertically from the other surfaces.

The standard prohibits the use of crossbraces as a means of access.

What types of access can be used?

Several types of access are permitted:

  • Ladders, such as portable, hook-on, attachable, and stairway
  • Stair towers
  • Ramps and walkways
  • Integral prefabricated frames

What are the access requirements for employees erecting and dismantling supported scaffolds?

Effective September 2, 1997, employees erecting and dismantling supported scaffolding must have a safe means of access provided when a competent person has determined the feasibility and analyzed the site conditions.
