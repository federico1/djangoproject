Three Workers Electrocuted

Case Reports from OSHA files

  • The victim was dismantling a tubular, welded-frame scaffold. Another employee, on the same level of the scaffold, was lowering planks to the next level while the victim was tying the scaffold frames to a pulley and lowering them to the ground. One of the frames became entangled with part of the scaffold structure. As he attempted to free the stuck scaffold frame, the victim fell approximately 24 feet to the ground, sustaining multiple skull fractures, brain contusions, and other internal injuries. He was taken to a hospital and pronounced dead on arrival. The scaffold frame that he was lowering came in contact with a 4,000-volt electric feeder line and caused an electric arc. It is not known if the explosion startled the victim and caused him to fall, or if he lost his balance before the explosion occurred.
  • A painter and a carpenter were painting church windows from a tubular, welded-frame scaffold approximately 25 feet high and mounted on rubber casters. After the workers had completed one side of the church, they attempted to move the scaffold to another location to continue painting. They passed the scaffold under a 12,000-volt power line that was approximately 30 feet above ground level. They then changed direction and tried to pass the scaffold under the same power line at a point where it was only 24 feet above ground level. The scaffold contacted one phase of the power line, providing a path to the ground for the electric current. The two workers grasping the scaffold were electrocuted.
  • Employees of a masonry company were erecting a brick wall from a tubular, welded-frame scaffold. The scaffold had been constructed approximately 21 inches across from a 7,620-volt power line. A laborer carried a 10' long x 8" wide piece of wire reinforcement along the top of the scaffold and contacted the power line with it. The laborer, who was wearing gloves, received an electric shock and dropped the wire reinforcement, which fell across the power line and simultaneously contacted the metal rail of the scaffold. A 20-year old bricklayer standing on the work platform in contact with the main scaffold was electrocuted.
